Meet The Board

Alan I. Zelazo - President

Alan recently retired after working 40 years on the railroad as a train Engineer for PATH since 1992. Prior to that, Al was a Towerman for Amtrak. He enjoys being an active member at The Shore Line Trolley Museum, a 501(c)(3) in East Haven, Connecticut, engaging in the restoration & preservation of vintage trolley & rapid transit cars. He is proud to serve as Vice Chairman on the Museum's Board of Trustees. Al is also the curator of the Hudson & Manhattan Car 503's restoration project, to bring this vintage train car back to its former glory. He is married to his wife Dolores of nearly 40 years and is the father to 2 children, Jonathan & Stephanie. He firmly believes in preserving our history for future generations which is why he wanted to become involved in this endeavor to preserve the Tappan Zee Bridge name for future generations.

Annette Brooks - Vice President

Growing up in a small Midwestern town along the Mississippi River ignited Annette’s lifelong love for bridges, rivers, waterway commerce, and recreation. It also influenced her appreciation for a rich river history intertwined with that the of early settlers and Native Americans. Along with the belief that the Tappan Zee history should be preserved, her childhood memories of life along a mighty river and the old bridge that spanned it, fuel her passion to help Save Our Tappan Zee. After earning undergraduate degrees from the University of Iowa and while enrolled in the MBA program at Portland State University in Portland, Oregon, Annette began to carve out a career in telecommunications. She went on to hold various executive positions, including Vice President, Switched Services Line of Business at Teleport Communications Group in New York and Vice President, Advanced Intelligent Networks at Nortel Networks in Richardson, Texas. Before she retired from the corporate world, Annette was Marketing Vice President/Chief Marketing Officer for venture-backed ($20-$30 million tranche) wireless portfolio companies, where she oversaw branding, product marketing, marketing communications, market analysis, and more. Throughout her life, Annette has been involved in various charities and nonprofit organizations. She was on the Plano Children’s Theatre Board of Directors when she lived in Dallas, and most recently, was a volunteer for the Wounded Warrior Project. Today, Annette, keeps busy as a successful freelance writer and marketer specializing in digital content, editorials, advertorials and social media marketing. She has called the greater New York/New Jersey metro area her permanent home since 2008, is married, and enjoys traveling the world and writing about her sojourns.

Raymond Ferrara - Board Member

Ray, married since 1969 to his wife Connie, as well as father and grandfather, attended high school in Elmsford, and graduated with a BBA from Pace College in 1968. He served his country with pride in the US Air Force from 1969-1971, achieving the rank of Sergeant. After starting a career with Chicago-based Vogue Tyre & Rubber Co in 1972 as a salesman, after 44 years, he retired as the Northeast Regional Vice President. Vogue Tyre was established in 1914 providing premium products to the automobile dealers as well as to the public. In 1974, Ray moved from salesman to sales manager; in 1976 he became General Manager; and in 1993, he was promoted to Vice President. He grew the company immensely by introducing innovative new procedures, by setting up satellite locales in the NY/NJ metro area, by negotiating 3 union contracts with the UAW, and for locating real estate for two 40,000 sq. ft. locations handling all renovations with the builders. Ray also has extensive board experience, as he currently serves on the board of the Westchester Seniors Golf Association which provides scholarships to caddies in the Westchester area.

Advisory Committee

Evan Pritchard - Committee Member

Evan Pritchard (Abachbahamitch in Mi’kmaq) is the Director of the Center for Algonquin Culture, and is the author of "Native New Yorkers", "Henry Hudson and the Algonquins of New York" (which is required reading for many high schools), "No Word For Time", and "Bird Medicine". He has also published extensively on the Lenape Indians, including "Introduction to Lenape Words and Phrases" and "Touring Native New York". His poetry book "Greetings from Mawenawasic" (Foothills, 2014) has been acclaimed by numerous leading Native American poets, features a forward by legendary Mohawk poet Susan Deer Cloud, and is featured in the book and film "Tending the Fire" by Chris Felver and also the play "Cedars", premiered at LaMama. He has worked with countless elders to help preserve the ancient history of North America, notably the late William Commanda. He was recently awarded the Helen Wilkinson Reynolds prize for his work unearthing forgotten Native American history in New York State. He has published a series of Native American history maps of the New York area, including one of Dutchess County. He has taught Native studies courses at Marist, Vassar, and Pace, with hundreds of radio and live appearances. An award-winning historian (Helen Wilkinson Reynolds Award, etc.), he is best known for his commentaries on the History Channel.
