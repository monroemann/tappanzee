- Meet The Board -

Dr. Dena Restaino, PT, DBT - Board Member

Dena is a lifelong resident of Westchester County for over 48 years, educated at Valhalla High, and then University of Miami, where she earned her BS as well as Master of Science in Physical Therapy. She then attended Des Moines Osteopathic School, where she became a Doctor of Physical Therapy, graduating with Honors and Distinction. She is a member of the Medical Honor Society since 2008. Past long-time employers include Burke Rehabilitation Hospital and St. Joseph's Medical Center. Today, she is self-employed at Kinetic Solutions. She takes great pride in treating any military veteran for free, encouraging any vet to contact her with any physical therapy issue he or she may have.

Raymond Ferrara - Board Member

Ray, married since 1969 to his wife Connie, as well as father and grandfather, attended high school in Elmsford, and graduated with a BBA from Pace College in 1968. He served his country with pride in the US Air Force from 1969-1971, achieving the rank of Sergeant. After starting a career with Chicago-based Vogue Tyre & Rubber Co in 1972 as a salesman, after 44 years, he retired as the Northeast Regional Vice President. Vogue Tyre was established in 1914 providing premium products to the automobile dealers as well as to the public. In 1974, Ray moved from salesman to sales manager; in 1976 he became General Manager; and in 1993, he was promoted to Vice President. He grew the company immensely by introducing innovative new procedures, by setting up satellite locales in the NY/NJ metro area, by negotiating 3 union contracts with the UAW, and for locating real estate for two 40,000 sq. ft. locations handling all renovations with the builders. Ray also has extensive board experience, as he currently serves on the board of the Westchester Seniors Golf Association which provides scholarships to caddies in the Westchester area.

Alan I. Zelazo - Board Member

Alan recently retired after working 40 years on the railroad as a train Engineer for PATH since 1992. Prior to that, Al was a Towerman for Amtrak. He enjoys being an active member at The Shore Line Trolley Museum, a 501(c)(3) in East Haven, Connecticut, engaging in the restoration & preservation of vintage trolley & rapid transit cars. He is proud to serve as Vice Chairman on the Museum's Board of Trustees. Al is also the curator of the Hudson & Manhattan Car 503's restoration project, to bring this vintage train car back to its former glory. He is married to his wife Dolores of nearly 40 years and is the father to 2 children, Jonathan & Stephanie. He firmly believes in preserving our history for future generations which is why he wanted to become involved in this endeavor to preserve the Tappan Zee Bridge name for future generations.
